# Project Overview

This project is a Digital Literacy Portfolio created as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course. The objective of this project is to develop essential digital skills required for academic and professional growth in today’s technology-driven world.

As a Student Digital Ambassador, this project focuses on helping students understand how to effectively and responsibly use digital tools and platforms. It covers various aspects such as creating awareness about digital literacy, building a professional online presence, exploring coding and collaboration platforms, practising professional communication, and understanding cyber safety.

The project is divided into five tasks, each targeting a specific area of digital competence. These tasks include designing an infographic, setting up professional profiles, solving coding challenges, creating collaborative tools, drafting formal emails, and analysing cybercrime scenarios.

Overall, this project reflects my learning journey, practical exposure, and understanding of how to navigate the digital world safely, efficiently, and professionally.
# Module Summaries

 Module 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness

This module introduced the fundamental concept of digital literacy and its growing importance in a technology-driven world. It covered key areas such as understanding digital tools, practising safe internet usage, maintaining a professional online presence, and following proper email etiquette. As part of this module, I created a visual infographic to simplify and communicate these concepts effectively to my peers. This helped me realise that digital literacy is not just about using technology, but also about using it responsibly, safely, and efficiently. It also improved my ability to present information creatively and in a user-friendly format.

 Module 2 – Building a Digital Portfolio

In this module, I learned how to establish a professional digital identity by creating and updating profiles on platforms such as GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. Each platform serves a unique purpose—GitHub for showcasing projects, LinkedIn for professional networking, and Kaggle for practising data science skills. This module helped me understand how recruiters and professionals evaluate online profiles. It also encouraged me to think about my long-term goals and how I can use these platforms to document my learning journey, build projects, and connect with others in the tech community. Overall, it marked the beginning of building my personal brand in the digital world.

 Module 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

This module focused on developing practical skills through coding practice and collaboration tools. I explored coding platforms like HackerRank/CodeChef, where I solved beginner-level problems to strengthen my logical thinking and programming basics. Additionally, I created a Google Form to design a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz, which demonstrated how data can be collected, organised, and analysed using Google Sheets. This module highlighted the importance of consistent practice in coding and the usefulness of collaborative tools in academic and professional environments. It also helped me become more comfortable with digital platforms used for teamwork and productivity.

 Module 4 – Email Etiquette & Online Communication

This module emphasised the importance of professional communication in the digital world. I learned how to write well-structured emails with clear subject lines, proper greetings, concise content, and appropriate sign-offs. Writing emails for real-life scenarios, such as requesting an extension or applying for an internship, gave me practical experience. Additionally, I created a checklist of do’s and don’ts for responsible social media use, which made me more aware of how online behaviour can impact personal and professional reputation. This module improved my communication skills and taught me how to interact respectfully and effectively in digital spaces.

 Module 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

This module focused on understanding various types of cybercrimes such as phishing, identity theft, and online fraud. I analysed a case study to understand how such crimes occur, who is targeted, and what consequences they have. I also created a prevention checklist with practical safety measures, especially focusing on financial and online security. This module made me more aware of the risks associated with digital platforms and the importance of being cautious while sharing information online. It encouraged me to adopt safer online habits and be more responsible as a digital user.
