SOCIAL MEDIA DO’s AND DONT’s CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS 

DO’s
•	Use strong and secure passwords
Create passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Change them regularly to keep your accounts safe. 
•	Keep your profiles professional
Use appropriate profile pictures, bios, and posts, especially on platforms like LinkedIn that reflect your academic and career identity. 
•	Think before posting
Always consider how your content might be perceived by others, including teachers, recruiters, or future employers. 
•	Verify information before sharing
Check the authenticity of news or information to avoid spreading misinformation or fake content. 
•	Respect others online
Be polite and respectful in comments and messages. Maintain a positive digital presence. 
•	Adjust privacy settings regularly
Review your account privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal information. 
•	Be mindful of your digital footprint
Remember that anything posted online can stay permanently and may impact your future opportunities. 




 DON’Ts
•	Don’t share personal information publicly
Avoid posting sensitive details like phone numbers, addresses, passwords, or financial information. 
•	Don’t click on suspicious links
Unknown links may lead to phishing or malware attacks. Always verify before clicking. 
•	Don’t post offensive or harmful content
Avoid content that is abusive, discriminatory, or inappropriate, as it can harm your reputation. 
•	Don’t overshare your location
Sharing your live location can compromise your safety. 
•	Don’t engage in cyberbullying
Never harass, threaten, or troll others online. It can have serious consequences. 
•	Don’t accept requests from unknown people
Fake accounts can misuse your data or attempt scams. 
•	Don’t ignore suspicious activity
If you notice unusual login attempts or messages, report and secure your account immediately.
